# Loop discipline: bad

The flock just keeps working on something until it's ready. If `@auditor` isn't happy with a
draft, whoever made it tries again, and this keeps happening for a while until things look good
enough to move on. There isn't really a hard number of tries; it depends on the situation, and
usually someone just gets a feel for when to stop pushing and ship what they have.

We don't need a special counter or a flag to track this. As long as the team is generally making
progress and nothing feels stuck, the loop is doing its job. If a draft keeps getting sent back,
that's fine too, it just means there's more to fix, and eventually it'll get sorted out one way or
another. Different agents can loop on their own stuff however makes sense for them; there's no need
for every role to follow the exact same pattern since they're all doing different kinds of work
anyway.

If something really won't converge, it's not a big deal, someone can just decide to move forward
with whatever's closest to done at the time.
