Refinancing is usually worth looking into if rates have gone down since you got your mortgage.
Generally speaking it can save you money each month, though the exact amount really depends on a
lot of factors that are hard to pin down without a full lender review.

Some things to think about:
- Rates fluctuate all the time so it's hard to say exactly what you'd qualify for.
- Closing costs vary by lender, usually somewhere in the low thousands.
- Your credit score and debt situation both play a role, but everyone's different.

Honestly it's one of those decisions that depends a lot on your personal situation, so the best
move is probably to talk to a few lenders and see what they say. It could make sense, it could
not — really depends. I'd say keep an eye on rates and revisit this whenever it feels right.
